Junk Removal Cost in Austin TX: What to Expect in 2025

April 18, 2026 • 5 min read • Junk Removal

Whether you are cleaning out a garage, getting rid of old furniture before a move, or dealing with an appliance that finally gave out, junk removal in Austin is a service most homeowners need at least once a year. Prices vary widely — from $75 for a single item to several hundred for a full cleanout. Here is what actually drives the cost, and what you should expect to pay.

How Junk Removal Is Priced in Austin

Most Austin junk removal services price jobs in one of three ways:

  • By volume (truck space used). National chains like 1-800-GOT-JUNK charge by how much of their truck your items fill. A quarter truck might cost $150–$250; a full truck can run $500–$700.
  • By item. Smaller local services like AssembleAtEase charge per item. This is usually cheaper for single-item pickups and more transparent overall — you know exactly what you'll pay upfront.
  • By weight. Less common in Austin but used by some haulers. The problem: you won't know the weight until after the job, making it hard to budget.

What Makes Austin Junk Removal More Expensive

Several factors can push your total higher than the base rate:

  • Stairs. Items on upper floors, rooftop decks, or in split-level homes in neighborhoods like Northwest Hills or Mueller require extra labor. Add $25–$50 per flight.
  • Heavy appliances. Refrigerators require two-person handling and special dollies. This is always factored in at AssembleAtEase but may be an added charge elsewhere.
  • Hazardous materials. Paint, chemicals, propane tanks, and old CRT televisions require special disposal. Not all junk removal services accept these — ask before booking.
  • Disassembly required. If a large sectional or wardrobe needs to be broken down before removal, that is additional labor. We include minor disassembly in our rates.
  • Travel distance. Central Austin pickups are easiest to schedule. Spicewood, Bastrop, Wimberley, and areas beyond 30 miles may carry a travel surcharge.

Things to Know Before You Schedule

  • Check donation options first. Items in decent condition — furniture, working appliances, clothing — can often be donated to Habitat for Humanity ReStore, Goodwill Austin, or Salvation Army. Some organizations offer free pickup, which will save you the removal fee entirely.
  • Craigslist free section. Post a "free — must take" listing the night before your scheduled removal. Austin has an active free-stuff community, and you might not need removal at all for some items.
  • Austin Resource Recovery bulk pickup. Austin residents can schedule up to two free bulk item pickups per month through Austin Resource Recovery for things like furniture and appliances. Scheduling is done online at austintexas.gov. The catch: pickup windows can be several weeks out, and items must be at the curb on specific days.
  • Same-day removal. Services like AssembleAtEase offer same-day junk removal in Austin when you book before noon. No waiting weeks for a municipal pickup.

Is Professional Junk Removal Worth It in Austin?

If you need something gone fast — before a move, after a renovation, or just because you can't live around it anymore — professional junk removal is almost always worth it. The average pickup takes under 30 minutes from arrival to departure. Renting a truck, loading it yourself, driving to the Austin Resource Recovery center at Hornsby Bend, and disposing of items there costs you 3–4 hours of your time and roughly $45–$65 in truck rental. For most people, $75–$95 for the convenience is a clear win.
