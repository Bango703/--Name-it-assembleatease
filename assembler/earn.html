Common questions
Pro FAQ
Do I need my own tools? ⌄
You'll need basic hand tools (screwdrivers, Allen keys, drill). We don't supply tools, but most assembly jobs only require a basic toolkit. When you apply, you'll indicate what tools you own.
How does payment work? ⌄
You keep 80% of the job price — AssembleAtEase retains a 20% platform fee. You see the exact take-home amount before you accept any job. Payouts are recorded by the owner and sent promptly after job completion.
Can I decline a job? ⌄
Absolutely. When the owner assigns you a job, you'll receive an email with accept/decline links. If you decline, the booking is returned to the owner for reassignment. We ask that you decline promptly so the customer isn’t kept waiting.
Do I need experience? ⌄
Prior assembly or handyman experience is strongly preferred, but not always required for Starter tier. We look for reliability, good communication, and a customer-first attitude as much as technical skill.
How long does approval take? ⌄
Most applications are reviewed within 48 hours. ID verification via Stripe Identity takes just a few minutes on your end. Once approved, you’ll receive a welcome email with your dashboard login link.
What if a customer is unhappy? ⌄
All disputes are handled through our owner. We have a dispute resolution process that protects both assemblers and customers. As long as you do quality work and communicate clearly, disputes are rare.
Is this a W-2 job or 1099? ⌄
Assemblers are independent contractors (1099). You set your own availability, accept the jobs you want, and are your own boss. No taxes are withheld from your pay — you receive the full job amount and are responsible for reporting and paying your own taxes.
What do I need to know about taxes as a 1099 contractor? ⌄
As an independent contractor, nothing is withheld from your pay. Here’s what you’re responsible for:

Self-employment tax (15.3%) — This covers Social Security and Medicare. W-2 employees split this with their employer; you pay the full amount yourself, but you can deduct half on your federal return.

Federal & state income tax — Texas has no state income tax, which is a real advantage. You still owe federal income tax on your net profit. Most contractors pay quarterly estimated taxes to the IRS (due Apr, Jun, Sep, Jan) to avoid a penalty at year-end.

Track your expenses — Tools, mileage driving to jobs, phone use for the app — these are deductible business expenses that reduce your taxable income.

1099-NEC form — If you earn $600 or more in a calendar year through AssembleAtEase, you’ll receive a 1099-NEC by January 31 for filing your taxes.

We recommend consulting a tax professional or using software like TurboTax Self-Employed or QuickBooks Self-Employed to stay organized. This is general information, not tax advice.
