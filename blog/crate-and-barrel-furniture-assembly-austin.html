How Much Does Crate and Barrel Furniture Assembly Cost in Austin, TX?

May 15, 2026 • 4 min read

If you've just picked up some beautiful pieces from Crate and Barrel at their Austin locations, you're probably wondering about the next step: assembly. Whether it's a West Lake Hills coffee table, a sleek dining set, or a bedroom dresser, professional assembly can save you hours of frustration and ensure everything is put together correctly.

Let's break down what you need to know about Crate and Barrel furniture assembly in Austin, plus when it makes sense to hire professionals like AssembleAtEase instead of going the DIY route.

Crate and Barrel Assembly: DIY vs. Professional Services

Crate and Barrel furniture is known for its quality and modern design, but assembly can be surprisingly time-consuming. A simple bookshelf might take 30 minutes to an hour, while a bed frame with a complex headboard could easily take two to three hours. Add in the stress of reading cryptic instruction manuals and hunting for the right tools, and suddenly professional assembly starts looking pretty appealing.

DIY costs you mainly time and potentially tools you don't have. If you're missing a screwdriver, Allen wrench, or power drill, you're adding to the bill in both money and effort. Professional assembly in Austin typically ranges from $75 to $200+ per piece, depending on complexity, though many Austin service providers offer bundled rates if you're assembling multiple items.

When You Should Hire a Professional in Austin

Not all furniture assembly is created equal. Here's when hiring a pro makes the most sense:

  • Complex pieces: Anything with electronics, multiple drawers, or intricate joinery. Crate and Barrel's mid-to-high-end dressers and entertainment units fall into this category.
  • Safety concerns: Large bookcases, wall units, or anything tall that needs to be securely anchored to prevent tipping. This is especially important if you have kids or pets in your Austin home.
  • You're short on time: Between work commutes on I-35 and life in the Austin area, many homeowners simply don't have the weekend bandwidth.
  • You don't have the right tools: Assembly often requires power tools like a drill or specific wrench sizes. Renting or buying these can add $30–$50 to your DIY costs.
  • You value your sanity: Honest assessment: some people find assembly genuinely stressful. If that's you, paying for peace of mind is worth it.

Money-Saving Tips for Crate and Barrel Assembly in Austin

Looking to save a few dollars? Here are some strategies Austin homeowners use:

  1. Bundle your assembly: If you're buying multiple pieces, get them all assembled at once. Professionals can often create package deals.
  2. Check if Crate and Barrel offers delivery assembly: Sometimes retailers include assembly in their delivery package, especially for larger purchases. It's worth asking.
  3. Schedule during off-peak hours: Weekday assemblies are sometimes cheaper than weekend appointments in Austin.
  4. Be ready when the pro arrives: Make sure you have a clear space, all parts unpacked, and the instruction manual ready. This prevents delays and extra charges.
