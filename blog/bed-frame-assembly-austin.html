Bed Frame Assembly in Austin TX: Cost, Time & What to Expect

April 15, 2026 • 5 min read • Furniture Assembly

Bed frame assembly is one of the most common requests we handle in Austin — and one of the most underestimated jobs homeowners attempt themselves. A basic metal frame takes 20 minutes. A platform bed with a slatted base, headboard, footboard, and storage drawers can take 2–3 hours and genuinely requires two people. Why Bed Assembly Takes Longer Than People Expect

Bed frames are deceptively complex. The box might say "Easy Assembly" but modern bed frames include:

  • Inset slat systems — 10–18 individual wooden slats that must be evenly spaced and properly seated
  • Headboard mounting — often involves bolts that must be accessed from inside the frame once it's partially assembled
  • Storage drawers — typically include individual metal glide rails that must be perfectly level and parallel
  • Two-person lifts — king-size frames require simultaneous lifting of both side rails while connecting center legs
  • Cam lock sequences — order of operations matters significantly, and assembling in the wrong order can require full disassembly to correct

Tips for a Smooth Bed Assembly

  • Clear the room before the assembler arrives. A queen platform bed needs roughly 10 feet of clear floor space during assembly. A king needs more. Move the old furniture and bedding first.
  • Confirm your box spring or mattress dimensions first. Especially for storage beds — some have side rails that sit 2" higher than standard, creating compatibility issues with certain mattresses.
  • Check for the C-wire on adjustable bases. Power adjustable bases (for split kings, etc.) require an accessible outlet near the head of the bed.
  • Inspect parts before the assembler starts. Bed frames have the highest rate of missing hardware among flat-pack furniture. Confirm the bag count before the job begins.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Tightening cam locks with a drill — they will strip and the frame will wobble
  • Assembling against a wall before checking that headboard mounting bolts are accessible
  • Forgetting the center support leg on king frames — over time this can cause slat sag in the middle
  • Skipping the slat securing tape on older IKEA frames — without it, slats can shift and fall through at 3am
