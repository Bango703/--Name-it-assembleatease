# AssembleAtEase Content Rules

These rules protect customer trust, booking conversion, and brand consistency.

## Hard Rules

- No emojis or emoji-style symbols in customer-facing platform copy or generated social content.
- Blog pages must stay short: maximum two body paragraphs before the booking CTA.
- Blog pages must not use "Get a Quote" as the main CTA. Use booking-focused language.
- Blog and social preview images must not repeat the logo as the article image.
- Do not duplicate article photos across the active blog index when enough distinct service images are available.
- Livelier social hooks are allowed, but never use fake crime claims, fake urgency, or scare tactics.
- Use "Blogs" as the public customer-facing label. Do not use "Guides" in nav, buttons, headings, or share prompts unless there is a specific legacy technical reason.
- Keep the public brand expandable beyond Austin. Do not make homepage, service pages, booking pages, dashboards, or global navigation sound like the company only exists in Austin.
- Keep Austin SEO in the background through metadata, schema, local blog content, Google Business posts, location pages, internal links, and service-area copy.
- Do not remove existing Austin SEO URLs without a redirect and canonical plan.
- Do not claim active service in a new city or state until operations, pricing, service area, and owner dispatch coverage are ready.
- Every generated content kit must include hashtags as a separate review field and inside the posts where the platform expects hashtags.

## Approved Social Hook Angles

- Security angle: smart locks, cameras, porch visibility, and before-you-need-it urgency without claiming a specific incident.
- Cost angle: what gets expensive when a job is done wrong or delayed.
- DIY mistake angle: common mistakes and what to check before starting.
- Local angle: city move-ins, apartments, weather, rentals, tight schedules, and local homes. Austin can be used when the post or page is specifically targeting Austin.
- Proof / trust angle: careful work, clean finish, verified pro, and job-done-right confidence.
- Direct offer angle: short, clear service offer with one practical booking benefit.

## Hashtag Rules

- Facebook: 1 to 3 hashtags, only if they look natural.
- Instagram: 6 to 10 hashtags with a mix of service, city, home setup, move-in, and brand tags.
- LinkedIn: 0 to 3 professional tags. Do not make LinkedIn look like Instagram.
- Google Business Profile: 0 to 2 hashtags maximum, and only if the post still reads clean.
- Always prefer specific hashtags over spam tags.
- Approved examples: `#FurnitureAssembly`, `#TVMounting`, `#SmartHomeSetup`, `#HomeSetup`, `#MoveInReady`, `#AustinTX`, `#AustinHome`, `#PropertyManagers`, `#AssembleAtEase`.
- Do not use scary hashtags, fake crime hooks, trend-chasing tags, unrelated viral tags, or competitor brand tags unless the blog is specifically about that brand or product type.

## City And SEO Rules

- Public front-end language should lead with the service and customer problem first, not the city first.
- Good front-end examples: "Furniture assembly without the weekend headache", "TV mounting that looks clean and stays secure", "Smart home setup before you need it."
- Avoid overusing city-first headlines like "Austin furniture assembly" on global sections. Save city-first language for local landing pages, blog titles, metadata, and Google Business Profile posts.
- Austin remains the launch market and SEO base, but the brand should be able to add Round Rock, Dallas, Houston, San Antonio, or another city without rewriting the whole site.
- Future city pages should be unique enough to avoid duplicate SEO pages. Each city page needs local service area details, scheduling notes, proof, photos where possible, and matching internal links.
- Keep all blog/share hooks professional and conversion-focused. The hook should create interest, not panic.
