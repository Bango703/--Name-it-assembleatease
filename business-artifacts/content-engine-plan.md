# AssembleAtEase Blog And SEO Content Engine Plan

Purpose: turn each blog post into bookings, social content, Google Business Profile posts, and apartment/property outreach without cluttering the website or trapping the brand inside one city.

## Naming Rule

- Public customer-facing label: Blogs.
- Do not use "Guides" in nav, page headings, buttons, share prompts, or owner-facing publish tools.
- Existing internal code may still use guide-related names until it is safe to rename, but visible copy should say Blogs.

## SEO Positioning Rule

AssembleAtEase launched in Austin, but the public brand should be city-expandable.

The front end should lead with:

- The service.
- The customer problem.
- The quality of the work.
- The booking path.

The front end should not lead every major section with:

- Austin.
- Austin TX.
- Austin homeowners.
- Austin-first language.

Austin SEO should stay active in the background through:

- Page titles and meta descriptions where the page is Austin-specific.
- JSON-LD schema serviceArea fields.
- Blog titles and bodies when the blog is targeting Austin search intent.
- Google Business Profile posts.
- Location pages.
- Image alt text where the image and page are truly Austin-specific.
- Internal links between Austin service pages, pricing, blogs, and booking.

Do not delete or rename current Austin URLs without a redirect, canonical, and search-risk plan.

## City Expansion Structure

Use this structure so the brand can expand city by city:

- Global/customer-facing pages: city-flexible copy, service-first language, no heavy Austin repetition.
- Austin local SEO pages: keep Austin targeting, Austin metadata, Austin schema, Austin internal links.
- Future city pages: add only when operations can actually serve that city.
- Service-area copy: show the active market clearly without making every headline city-locked.

Good visible examples:

- "Book furniture assembly, TV mounting, and smart home setup."
- "Pick the service, choose what you need, and we confirm the job."
- "Clean setup help for homes, apartments, offices, and move-ins."

Use Austin-specific examples mostly in blogs, location sections, footer service area text, metadata, and Google Business content.

## Operating Order

1. Publish or update one blog on the website.
2. Decide whether the blog is global, Austin-local, suburb-local, business, or seasonal.
3. Link the blog to the matching service page and booking path.
4. Link the matching service page back to related blogs.
5. Create the social kit from the blog.
6. Add share hooks so the blog can send traffic out and bring traffic back in.
7. Post manually first: Facebook, Google Business Profile, Instagram, LinkedIn.
8. Reuse the same blog as a short video script.
9. Reuse the same blog as an apartment/property outreach talking point when relevant.
10. Track clicks, bookings, phone calls, quote requests, and Buffer post results from that blog.
11. Update weak blogs every 60 to 90 days with better pricing, local examples, photos, FAQs, and stronger hooks.
12. Only automate posting after the real social profiles, permissions, API keys, and Buffer settings are approved.

## Full Blog Pipeline

The full pipeline is:

1. Pick topic: service-first, Austin-local, suburb-local, seasonal, or business.
2. Confirm booking path: every blog needs one matching service or quote path before writing.
3. Choose image: no duplicated active blog image when enough service photos exist.
4. Write blog: maximum two body paragraphs before the booking CTA.
5. Add SEO background: title, meta description, canonical, Open Graph, image alt, local schema when relevant.
6. Add internal links: matching service page, pricing, one related blog, and booking.
7. Add share block: Facebook, LinkedIn, copy link, and email. Add SMS only on mobile if it stays clean.
8. Generate content kit: Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Google Business, video script, apartment outreach, and hashtags.
9. Review hashtags: keep them specific, clean, and platform-appropriate.
10. Test Buffer: use dry run first and inspect channel payloads.
11. Publish to Buffer queue: Facebook and Google Business first, then add other channels only when ready.
12. Track history: owner dashboard should show selected, generated, tested, queued, emailed, and failed states.
13. Measure traffic: clicks, UTM source, bookings, calls, quote requests, and weak posts.
14. Refresh: update the blog and social hook every 60 to 90 days if it underperforms.

## Required Links

Every article should include:

- Service page link: current matching service page, business page, or booking path.
- Booking link: `/book` or the matching `/book?service=...` path.
- Social share links: Facebook and LinkedIn first. Add X, Threads, SMS, and email only if they stay clean on mobile.
- Copy link button with tracking-ready URL.
- One follow link: current Facebook profile until Instagram and LinkedIn company profiles are live.
- UTM source for posts generated from the website or Buffer.

Every service page should include:

- Three related blogs when enough good blogs exist.
- One "All blogs" link.
- No discontinued service links.

## Social Kit Template

Use this every time a blog is published.

Website post:

- Title: service problem plus booking intent. Add the city only when the post is meant to rank locally.
- CTA: book the matching service.
- Internal links: matching service page, pricing, and one related blog.
- Share block: "Share this with someone setting up a home, rental, office, or move-in."

Facebook post:

- Hook: practical local or service-specific first line.
- 2 to 4 short lines from the article.
- CTA: "Read the blog or book a visit."
- Link: article URL.

Instagram caption:

- Hook in first line.
- 3 practical bullets.
- CTA: "Save this before your next setup project."
- Link-in-bio target: article URL or booking page.

LinkedIn post:

- Angle: apartments, property managers, small offices, or local operations.
- Explain the business problem: time, tenant readiness, repeat work, fewer missed appointments.
- CTA: "For move-ins and office setups, see the blog." Use the city only when targeting a local post.

Google Business Profile post:

- Keep it direct and local for the active Google Business location.
- Mention Austin and nearby suburbs naturally while Austin is the active launch market.
- CTA: "Book online" or "Learn more."

Short video script:

- 0-3 sec: show the problem.
- 3-12 sec: explain the mistake or cost.
- 12-22 sec: show the better way.
- 22-30 sec: CTA to book or read the blog.

Blog share hooks:

- "Moving soon? Send this to the person ordering the furniture."
- "Before the TV goes on the wall, check these first."
- "Save this before your next setup project."
- "Use this before you book a multi-item setup."
- "Send this to a property manager, roommate, or client who needs the room ready."

Do not use fake break-in claims, fake urgent warnings, or scary crime language. Security hooks are allowed only when they are practical: locks, cameras, doorbell placement, porch visibility, Wi-Fi, and setup readiness.

Hashtag kit:

- Return a separate hashtag set for owner review.
- Include Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Google Business recommendations separately.
- Keep Austin tags for Austin-local posts and Google Business Profile posts.
- Use service-first tags for global posts so the brand can expand later.
- Do not overload captions just because hashtags exist.

Apartment outreach talking point:

- "We help residents get moved in faster by handling furniture assembly, TV mounting, and smart home setup in one appointment."
- Mention reduced resident frustration, cleaner move-ins, and easier leasing office referrals.

## Blog Topic Strategy

Use a mix of national/service-intent topics and local SEO topics.

Ratio for launch:

- 60 percent service-first topics that can work in any city.
- 30 percent Austin or nearby suburb SEO topics.
- 10 percent business/property manager topics.

When expanding:

- Keep the strongest service-first blogs as reusable brand content.
- Create unique local blog versions only for cities where AssembleAtEase can actually complete jobs.
- Do not mass-create thin city-swap blogs.

## Blog Sharing And Traffic Loop

Every active blog should support:

- Clean share buttons: Facebook, LinkedIn, copy link, email.
- Optional SMS share on mobile if it does not clutter the page.
- UTM tracking on generated share URLs.
- Open Graph title, description, image, and canonical URL.
- Twitter/X card metadata even if X is not a priority channel.
- A short reusable hook for Buffer posts.
- A booking CTA near the first two paragraphs.
- Related blogs that keep the reader moving through service intent.

The goal is two-way traffic:

- Send blog content out through Buffer, social posts, email, SMS, and apartment outreach.
- Bring readers back to a specific booking path, not just the homepage.

## Automation Gate

Do not turn on automatic social posting until:

- Buffer API key is added as `BUFFER_API_KEY`.
- Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, and Google Business Profile are connected inside Buffer.
- Buffer channel IDs are added for the channels you want to publish to.
- UTM tracking is decided.
- Buffer queue/review settings are confirmed so posts do not go out at messy times.
