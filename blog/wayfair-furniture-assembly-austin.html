Wayfair Furniture Assembly in Austin TX: Cost and Tips

April 16, 2026 • 5 min read • Furniture Assembly

Wayfair has become one of the most popular furniture retailers in Austin — and for good reason. The selection is vast, prices are competitive, and delivery is usually fast. But assembly? That is where Wayfair's value proposition starts to wobble. Their in-house "White Glove" assembly add-on is pricier than most local alternatives, and the quality of the contractor they send can be hit-or-miss. Here is what you should know about Wayfair assembly before you click "Order."

Wayfair's Built-In Assembly Service

Wayfair offers assembly add-ons at checkout for many items — particularly beds, dressers, and office furniture. The service is outsourced to national contractors who operate across Austin. The cost is typically $69–$199 depending on the item, and the experience is bundled into your delivery window. Convenience aside, there are a few downsides:

  • Assembly is contracted to third parties — Wayfair does not directly control quality
  • If there is a problem with the assembly, Wayfair customer service and the assembly company point at each other
  • Scheduling is locked to the delivery window — you cannot easily rebook for a different day
  • The contractors are typically paid by the piece, which means speed is prioritized over quality

What to Do When Your Wayfair Order Arrives

Before you even open the boxes, do the following to protect yourself:

  • Inspect packaging for visible damage. If a box has a large dent, crush, or tear, take a photo before the delivery driver leaves. Damaged packaging often means damaged furniture.
  • Note any hardware that is missing. Wayfair's quality control is generally decent, but missing hardware bags are not uncommon. Check the parts list and count the bags before assembly begins.
  • Check the manual immediately. Particularly for beds and wardrobes, confirm you have the right model and all components before the assembler arrives — it saves a trip if something is wrong.
  • Contact Wayfair the same day for damages. Their policy allows returns and replacements, but only if you report damage within a short window of delivery.

Wayfair Assembly Challenges Our Techs See Most Often

Our Austin assembly techs work with Wayfair furniture every day. Here are the most common issues:

  • Cam locks that strip easily. Wayfair uses cam-and-dowel connectors heavily. Over-tightening strips them immediately. Proper hand-tightening technique is important — power drills should not be used.
  • Mismatched hole placements. Particularly on imported furniture, pre-drilled holes can be 2–3mm off. A professional knows how to handle this without damaging the piece.
  • Missing drawer slides. Drawer slides are often the most complicated part of Wayfair furniture assembly and the most frequently installed incorrectly, causing drawers that bind or fall open.
  • Veneer damage from poor tools. Wayfair furniture uses MDF/veneer surfaces. Using the wrong driver or overtightening hardware can mar the surface permanently.

Tips for a Smooth Wayfair Assembly

  • Schedule assembly for the day after delivery — this gives you time to inspect the packaging and confirm all parts are present
  • Clear the room where the furniture will live before the assembler arrives — flat-pack boxes take significant floor space during assembly
  • Have a small Phillips head screwdriver handy — some Wayfair pieces have hardware that works better with a manual driver than the provided Allen wrench
  • For large wardrobes and beds, confirm someone else will be home — an extra pair of hands is often needed even for pros
