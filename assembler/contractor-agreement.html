Independent Contractor Agreement

Between AssembleAtEase and the Easer Applicant

AssembleAtEase • Austin, Texas

PARTIES. This Independent Contractor Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the date of electronic acceptance, between AssembleAtEase, a Texas-based marketplace platform operating at assembleatease.com ("Company" or "Platform"), and the individual who applies to and registers as an "Easer" on the AssembleAtEase platform ("Contractor"). By electronically accepting this Agreement during the application process, the parties agree to be bound by the terms set forth herein.

Section 1

Independent Contractor Relationship

1.1 The Contractor is an independent contractor and not an employee, partner, joint venturer, agent, or representative of the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create an employment relationship, partnership, joint venture, franchise, or agency relationship between the parties.
1.2 The Contractor shall have full control over the manner and means of performing services, subject only to the result requirements specified by customers and the Platform's quality standards and Code of Conduct. The Contractor determines their own schedule, hours, and methods of work.
1.3 The Company shall not have the right to direct the Contractor as to the specific hours of work, the order in which work shall be performed, or the precise means by which results are accomplished, beyond specifying the customer-required outcome and quality standards.
1.4 The Contractor acknowledges and agrees that they are not entitled to any employee benefits from the Company, including but not limited to: health, dental, or vision insurance; retirement or 401(k) benefits; paid time off, vacation, or sick leave; disability insurance; workers' compensation as an employee; unemployment insurance; or any other employee benefit program.
1.5 The Contractor may work for and provide services to other companies, platforms, or individuals simultaneously, provided such work does not directly conflict with the Contractor's obligations under this Agreement or violate any non-solicitation provisions herein.

Important Notice: You are applying as an independent contractor, not an employee. You are solely responsible for your own taxes, insurance, equipment, and compliance with all applicable laws. This is not an employment agreement.

Section 2

Services and Scope of Work

2.1 Service Categories. The Contractor agrees to provide the following types of services to customers of the AssembleAtEase platform as assigned, subject to the Contractor's acceptance of individual job offers:
(a)Furniture Assembly — flat-pack and ready-to-assemble furniture including IKEA, Wayfair, Amazon, and similar brands;
(b)TV and Display Mounting — wall mounting of televisions, monitors, and displays;
(c)Smart Home Installation — setup and installation of smart devices, hubs, cameras, and connected home equipment;
(d)Fitness Equipment Assembly — assembly of exercise machines, weight racks, and fitness equipment;
(e)Outdoor and Playset Assembly — assembly of outdoor furniture, playsets, trampolines, and related structures;
(f)Office Assembly — assembly of office furniture, shelving, and workspace equipment.
2.2 Job Acceptance. The Contractor is not required to accept any particular job offer and retains the right to decline individual assignments. However, a sustained pattern of declining job offers will negatively affect the Contractor's dispatch priority score and tier status on the Platform, potentially resulting in fewer or no job opportunities.
2.3 Completion Standard. Once the Contractor accepts a job offer, they agree to complete the assigned work in a professional, workmanlike manner consistent with industry standards and in accordance with the Platform's Code of Conduct and quality requirements. Accepted jobs shall not be abandoned without prior authorization from the Company.
2.4 Service Area. All services are performed for customers located in the Austin, Texas metropolitan area, including ZIP codes 786xx through 788xx. The Company may expand or modify the service area at any time with notice to the Contractor.
2.5 No Exclusivity. This Agreement does not grant the Contractor exclusive rights to any geographic area or service category. The Company may contract with multiple Easers for the same services in the same areas.
Section 3

Compensation and Platform Fees

3.1 Job Compensation. The Contractor will be compensated based on the total gross job amount minus the applicable Platform Fee. The Contractor's net earnings for each job ("Contractor Earnings") equal: Gross Job Amount − Platform Fee.
3.2 Platform Fee Schedule. The Company deducts a platform fee from the gross job amount to cover marketplace operations, customer acquisition, payment processing, dispatch technology, insurance costs, and support infrastructure. The current fee schedule is as follows:
Contractor Status Platform Fee Contractor Earns
Standard (No Membership) 35% 65% of gross job amount
Membership Contractor 25% 75% of gross job amount
3.3 Fee Changes. The Company reserves the right to modify the platform fee schedule with thirty (30) days' prior written notice to active Contractors. Continued acceptance of job offers after the effective date of any fee change constitutes acceptance of the revised fee schedule.
3.4 Membership. Contractors may optionally purchase a platform membership to reduce their platform fee. Membership fees are non-refundable except as required by applicable law. Membership status may affect dispatch priority in addition to fee rates.
3.5 Application Fee. AssembleAtEase may charge a one-time application fee to cover identity verification processing and application review. During the Founding Easer launch program, this fee may be waived, deferred, or otherwise credited at AssembleAtEase's discretion. If an application fee is charged, the fee terms shown at submission control.

Application Fee Policy: If an application fee is charged at submission, the displayed fee policy applies. If the Founding Easer launch program waives the fee, no application payment is collected at submission.

3.6 Payment Method. Payments to the Contractor are made manually by the Company through mutually agreed-upon methods, which may include bank transfer, check, Zelle, or other methods. The Company does not use automated payout rails or Stripe Connect. All Contractor payouts are recorded in the Platform's payout ledger and visible in the Easer dashboard.
3.7 Payment Schedule. Payments are typically issued within seven (7) business days after job completion and customer payment capture. The Company will notify the Contractor of available payout amounts via the Easer dashboard and email. The Contractor is responsible for confirming that their payment information on file is accurate and up to date.
3.8 Disputes About Compensation. The Contractor must raise any dispute about compensation in writing within thirty (30) days of the date the disputed payment was or was not made. Disputes not raised within this period are waived.
3.9 No Guarantee of Work or Income. The Company makes no guarantee of any minimum number of jobs, hours, or earnings. The availability of work depends on customer demand, the Contractor's availability, performance metrics, geographic location, tier status, and other market factors beyond the Company's control. The Contractor understands that income from the Platform may be irregular and unpredictable.
Section 4

Taxes and Withholding

4.1 Contractor's Tax Responsibility. As an independent contractor, the Contractor is solely and exclusively responsible for all federal, state, and local income taxes, self-employment taxes (Social Security and Medicare), and any other taxes or assessments arising from compensation received under this Agreement.
4.2 No Withholding. The Company will not withhold any income taxes, Social Security taxes, Medicare taxes, or other federal, state, or local taxes from compensation paid to the Contractor. The Contractor is responsible for making estimated quarterly tax payments to the Internal Revenue Service and the Texas Comptroller (if applicable) as required by law.
4.3 Form 1099-NEC. The Company will issue the Contractor a Form 1099-NEC if total compensation paid during a calendar year equals or exceeds $600.00, as required by applicable federal law. The Contractor is responsible for reporting all income, including amounts below the $600 reporting threshold.
4.4 Form W-9. The Contractor agrees to provide the Company with a completed and accurate IRS Form W-9 upon request and within ten (10) business days of such request. The Contractor agrees to promptly notify the Company of any change in their tax identification number, legal name, or business entity status.
4.5 Indemnification for Tax Liability. The Contractor agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Company from any liability, claims, assessments, penalties, fines, or interest arising from the Contractor's failure to pay any taxes owed on compensation received under this Agreement.

Tax Notice: As a 1099 independent contractor, you should expect to owe self-employment tax of approximately 15.3% on your net earnings, plus applicable federal and state income taxes. We recommend consulting a tax professional to understand your estimated tax obligations.

Section 5

Tools, Equipment, and Transportation

5.1 The Contractor is solely responsible for providing all tools, equipment, materials, and transportation necessary to perform the services described in this Agreement. The Company does not supply tools, vehicles, fuel, or any other operational resources.
5.2 The Contractor represents and warrants that they possess, or will possess prior to performing any job, the tools, equipment, and reliable transportation necessary to perform the services they have indicated in their application profile.
5.3 The Contractor is responsible for maintaining their tools and equipment in safe, functional, and good working condition. The Contractor shall replace or repair any tools or equipment that become unsafe, worn, defective, or otherwise unfit for professional use.
5.4 All costs and expenses related to tools, equipment, transportation, fuel, parking, tolls, and supplies are the sole responsibility of the Contractor. The Company will not reimburse any such expenses unless expressly agreed upon in writing in advance.
5.5 The Contractor shall not use subcontractors or helpers on any job without prior written consent from the Company. Only the approved Contractor may perform services under this Agreement.
Section 6

Identity Verification and Background Requirements

6.1 Identity Verification Requirement. As a condition of activation and the right to receive job offers, the Contractor must complete identity verification through Stripe Identity, a third-party identity verification service operated by Stripe, Inc. The Contractor authorizes the collection, use, and processing of their government-issued identification documents and biometric data for the purpose of verifying their identity.
6.2 Background Representations. The Contractor represents and warrants that, to the best of their knowledge:
(a)They have not been convicted of any felony offense within the past seven (7) years;
(b)They have not been convicted of any crime involving theft, fraud, dishonesty, violence, sexual misconduct, or harm to persons within the past seven (7) years;
(c)They are not currently subject to any criminal charges, indictment, or pending proceedings for any offense listed in (a) or (b) above;
(d)They are legally authorized to work in the United States and are at least eighteen (18) years of age.
6.3 Ongoing Disclosure Obligation. The Contractor agrees to immediately notify the Company in writing if, during the term of this Agreement, they are arrested, charged with, or convicted of any criminal offense. Failure to make such disclosure within forty-eight (48) hours of the event constitutes a material breach of this Agreement and grounds for immediate termination.
6.4 Right to Verify. The Company reserves the right to conduct background checks, criminal history searches, reference checks, or other verification procedures at any time during the term of this Agreement, at the Company's expense. The Contractor's consent to such checks is a condition of maintaining active status on the Platform.
6.5 Misrepresentation. Any misrepresentation or omission in the Contractor's application or in any representation made under this Section constitutes a material breach of this Agreement and grounds for immediate termination without refund of any fees paid.
Section 7

Standards of Professional Conduct

7.1 Professional Standards. The Contractor agrees at all times to maintain the highest standards of professionalism in all interactions with customers, the Company, and Company representatives. Without limiting the foregoing, the Contractor specifically agrees to:
(a)Arrive to all accepted jobs on time and notify the customer and the Company promptly via the Platform if a delay is unavoidable;
(b)Treat customers, their family members, their property, and their homes with respect, care, and courtesy at all times;
(c)Complete all accepted work to the specifications agreed upon with the customer and to professional quality standards;
(d)Maintain a clean and organized work area throughout the job and thoroughly clean up all debris, packaging, and materials upon completion;
(e)Not bring additional persons, subcontractors, or helpers to job sites without prior written approval from the Company;
(f)Not engage in harassing, discriminatory, threatening, or unprofessional conduct of any kind toward customers or Company personnel;
(g)Not consume alcohol, controlled substances, or any substance that impairs judgment or performance while on the way to or at any job site;
(h)Wear appropriate attire and maintain a professional appearance at all job sites;
(i)Carry and use appropriate tools in a safe manner and in accordance with manufacturer guidelines;
(j)Use the AssembleAtEase Platform application and communications system as the designated channel for all job-related communications;
(k)Not take or retain photographs of customers' homes, possessions, or family members without their express permission.
7.2 Platform-Only Communication and Payments. The Contractor agrees that all communications with customers related to jobs booked through the Platform shall be conducted exclusively through the Platform's official messaging system. The Contractor shall not share personal email addresses, phone numbers, or social media accounts with customers for job-related purposes. The Contractor shall not solicit, accept, or request cash, Venmo, Zelle, or any other direct payment from customers for jobs booked through the Platform.
7.3 Performance Metrics. The Contractor acknowledges that customers may submit ratings and written reviews following each job. The Company uses performance metrics including average customer rating, job completion rate, acceptance rate, and response time to determine the Contractor's tier classification and dispatch priority. Poor performance metrics may result in fewer job offers, tier demotion, suspension, or termination of this Agreement.
7.4 Property Damage. The Contractor agrees to exercise reasonable professional care to avoid damage to customer property. The Contractor shall immediately report any property damage caused during a job to both the customer and the Company through the Platform. The Contractor shall cooperate fully with any Company investigation of property damage claims.
Section 8

Customer Data and Privacy

8.1 The Contractor acknowledges that in the course of performing services through the Platform, they will have access to customer personal information including, without limitation, names, home addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, and information about customers' residences and possessions ("Customer Data").
8.2 Confidentiality Obligations. The Contractor agrees to treat all Customer Data as strictly confidential. The Contractor shall not:
(a)Use Customer Data for any purpose other than performing the assigned services;
(b)Disclose Customer Data to any third party for any reason;
(c)Retain Customer Data beyond what is necessary for the completion of the relevant job;
(d)Use Customer Data to contact customers outside the Platform for any commercial, personal, or other purpose;
(e)Use Customer Data to attempt to circumvent the Platform for direct business solicitation.
8.3 The Contractor agrees to comply with all applicable privacy and data protection laws, including the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act, with respect to any Customer Data accessed during the performance of services. The Contractor shall promptly notify the Company if they become aware of any unauthorized access to, disclosure of, or loss of Customer Data.
8.4 The Contractor's obligations regarding Customer Data shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years.
Section 9

Intellectual Property

9.1 All work product, deliverables, and physical results produced by the Contractor for customers in connection with services performed under this Agreement shall be the property of the respective customer upon completion and full payment.
9.2 The Contractor grants the Company a non-exclusive, royalty-free, perpetual, worldwide license to use the Contractor's name, profile photo (if provided), ratings, reviews, and aggregated performance data generated through Platform activities for marketing, operational, and promotional purposes.
9.3 The Contractor shall not use the AssembleAtEase name, logo, brand marks, trademarks, trade dress, or any other proprietary intellectual property of the Company for any purpose without prior written authorization from the Company, except to identify themselves as a Platform-approved Contractor to customers in the course of performing assigned services.
9.4 All information, processes, systems, software, and technology provided by the Company in connection with the Platform are and shall remain the sole property of the Company. The Contractor acquires no ownership interest in any Company intellectual property through this Agreement.
Section 10

Insurance and Liability

10.1 Contractor's Insurance Responsibility. The Contractor is strongly encouraged to obtain and maintain, at their own expense, adequate general liability insurance covering property damage and personal injury claims arising from or related to the performance of services. The Contractor acknowledges that the Company does not provide workers' compensation, general liability, professional liability, or any other insurance coverage for the Contractor or any work performed by the Contractor.
10.2 Automobile Insurance. If the Contractor uses a motor vehicle in connection with performing services, the Contractor agrees to maintain valid automobile liability insurance meeting at least the minimum requirements of the State of Texas.
10.3 Liability for Damage. The Contractor is personally responsible and financially liable for any damage to customer property caused by the Contractor's negligence, carelessness, willful misconduct, or failure to perform services in a workmanlike manner. The Contractor agrees to cooperate with the Company's investigation of all property damage claims and to respond to such claims within forty-eight (48) hours of notification.
10.4 Limitation of Liability. To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, the Company's total cumulative liability to the Contractor arising out of or related to this Agreement, regardless of the theory of liability, shall not exceed the total net compensation actually paid by the Company to the Contractor during the three (3) calendar months immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim.
10.5 Consequential Damages. Neither party shall be liable to the other for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, exemplary, or punitive damages, including lost profits or loss of business opportunity, arising out of or related to this Agreement, even if such party has been advised of the possibility of such damages.
10.6 Indemnification by Contractor. The Contractor agrees to indemnify, defend (at the Company's option), and hold harmless the Company, its owners, officers, directors, employees, agents, successors, and assigns from and against any and all claims, demands, lawsuits, damages, losses, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs) arising out of or resulting from:
(a)The Contractor's performance of or failure to perform services under this Agreement;
(b)Any breach of this Agreement, representation, or warranty by the Contractor;
(c)The Contractor's negligence, gross negligence, willful misconduct, or fraud;
(d)Any violation of applicable law, regulation, or third-party right by the Contractor;
(e)Any claim by a government authority regarding the Contractor's tax obligations or employment classification.
Section 11

Non-Solicitation

11.1 Non-Solicitation of Customers. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months following its termination or expiration for any reason, the Contractor agrees not to directly or indirectly solicit, accept, or perform assembly, mounting, smart home, fitness, outdoor, or office services for any customer that was first introduced to the Contractor through the Platform, other than through the Platform itself and subject to the Platform's fees.
11.2 Non-Solicitation of Easers. During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months following its termination, the Contractor agrees not to solicit, recruit, or induce any other Platform Contractor to terminate their relationship with the Company or to perform services competing with the Company's business.
11.3 Reasonable Limitation. This non-solicitation provision is expressly limited to customers introduced through the Platform and does not prohibit the Contractor from: (a) providing services to customers they independently knew and had a pre-existing business relationship with prior to joining the Platform; (b) advertising their services generally to the public through their own independent channels; or (c) accepting work from customers who independently contact the Contractor through channels entirely unrelated to the Platform after the applicable restriction period.
11.4 Remedies. The Contractor acknowledges that a violation of this Section would cause the Company irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy, and the Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and any other legal or equitable remedy available, in addition to any damages.
Section 12

Term and Termination

12.1 Term. This Agreement is effective on the date of electronic acceptance and, unless sooner terminated in accordance with this Section, shall remain in effect continuously until terminated by either party.
12.2 Termination by Contractor. The Contractor may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing seven (7) days' prior written notice to the Company at service@assembleatease.com. The Contractor must complete all accepted and in-progress jobs prior to the effective date of termination or arrange for their reassignment through the Company.
12.3 Immediate Termination for Cause. The Company may terminate this Agreement immediately and without prior notice, effective upon the Company's delivery of written notice to the Contractor, for cause, including but not limited to any of the following events:
(a)Material violation of the Platform's Code of Conduct or quality standards;
(b)Material breach of any term of this Agreement;
(c)Fraud, dishonesty, misrepresentation, or intentional misconduct;
(d)Criminal arrest, charge, or conviction for any offense described in Section 6.2;
(e)Multiple credible customer complaints indicating a pattern of unprofessional, unsafe, or substandard conduct;
(f)Abandonment of accepted jobs without authorization on two or more occasions;
(g)Solicitation of customers or other Easers in violation of Section 11;
(h)Any other conduct that the Company reasonably determines poses a material risk to the safety of customers, the integrity of the Platform, or the Company's reputation.
12.4 Termination Without Cause. The Company may also terminate this Agreement without cause upon fourteen (14) days' prior written notice to the Contractor. No additional compensation beyond earned amounts for completed jobs shall be owed upon termination without cause.
12.5 Effect of Termination. Upon the effective date of termination: (a) the Contractor's access to the Platform will be deactivated; (b) the Company will pay all undisputed, earned compensation for fully completed jobs within fourteen (14) business days; (c) the Contractor's license to use the Company's intellectual property is immediately revoked; and (d) all provisions of this Agreement that by their nature should survive termination—including without limitation Sections 4 (Taxes), 8 (Privacy), 9 (IP), 10 (Liability), 11 (Non-Solicitation), 13 (Governing Law), and 14 (Miscellaneous)—shall survive and remain in full force and effect.
Section 13

Disputes and Governing Law

13.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by, construed, and enforced in accordance with the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to its conflict of law principles or rules. The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods does not apply to this Agreement.
13.2 Exclusive Venue. Subject to the arbitration provision below, any legal action, proceeding, or claim arising out of or related to this Agreement that is not subject to arbitration shall be brought exclusively in the state or federal courts of competent jurisdiction located in Travis County, Texas. The parties irrevocably consent to the personal jurisdiction of such courts and waive any objection to venue in such courts, including any objection based on inconvenient forum.
13.3 Mandatory Informal Resolution. Before initiating any formal dispute resolution proceeding, the party asserting a claim must first provide written notice to the other party describing the dispute in reasonable detail and the relief sought. The parties agree to negotiate in good faith for a period of thirty (30) days following receipt of such notice before either party may initiate arbitration or litigation.
13.4 Binding Arbitration. Except for claims seeking injunctive or other equitable relief, any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or related to this Agreement, or the breach, termination, or validity thereof, that cannot be resolved informally pursuant to Section 13.3, shall be submitted to and resolved by binding arbitration. Such arbitration shall be conducted by a single arbitrator in Austin, Texas, in accordance with the Commercial Arbitration Rules of the American Arbitration Association in effect at the time the arbitration is initiated. The award rendered by the arbitrator shall be final, binding, and non-appealable, and judgment upon the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.
13.5 Arbitration Costs. Each party shall bear its own costs and attorneys' fees in connection with arbitration, unless the arbitrator determines that a party's position was frivolous or in bad faith, in which case the arbitrator may award reasonable attorneys' fees and costs to the prevailing party.
13.6 Class Action Waiver. The Contractor expressly waives any right to bring or participate in any class action lawsuit, class-wide arbitration, collective action, representative action, or multi-plaintiff proceeding against the Company. All claims must be brought on an individual basis only.

14.10 Counterparts. This Agreement may be executed in one or more counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original and all of which together shall constitute one and the same instrument. Electronic or digital execution is equally valid and binding.
