5 Home Repairs Austin Homeowners Should Never DIY

April 17, 2026 • 5 min read • Home Repairs

There is a version of every home repair that looks easy on YouTube and becomes a weekend-destroying nightmare in real life. Austin homeowners — many of whom are in newly purchased homes or new-to-Texas transplants unfamiliar with local building quirks — often underestimate how quickly a simple job can go wrong. Here are five repairs where the cost of getting it wrong far outweighs the cost of hiring a professional.

1 Drywall Repair in Textured Walls

Patching a hole in drywall sounds trivial. Buy a patch kit, spackle, sand, paint. Done in an afternoon. Except Austin homes — particularly homes built in the 1980s through 2000s across neighborhoods like Round Rock, Cedar Park, and Pflugerville — typically have orange peel or knockdown texture on their walls. Matching this texture is a genuine skill. A patched section with the wrong texture stands out like a scar under raked light, morning sun, or when you're preparing to sell. Most homeowners spend a weekend making it worse before calling a pro. A professional charges $75–$135 for a patch and blended texture match. That is the right call.

2 Fixing a Sagging or Sticking Door

Austin's clay-heavy soil causes significant foundation movement year-round. Doors that stick in summer and gap in winter are extremely common. The temptation is to plane down the door — but the real issue is usually the frame, the hinges, or the foundation itself. Planing a door that is sticking because of seasonal expansion will leave you with a gap-prone door in winter. A handyman can diagnose the actual cause, adjust hinges, reattach strike plates, or recommend foundation evaluation when necessary. The diagnostic visit alone saves most homeowners from making the wrong fix.

3 Installing or Replacing a Faucet (if you have old plumbing)

Modern faucet replacement is fairly DIY-friendly in newer homes. But much of Central and East Austin has plumbing from the 1940s–1970s with galvanized steel pipes that corrode and seize. Attempting to disconnect an old supply line without the right tools or knowledge can result in a cracked pipe inside the wall — turning a $95 faucet install into a $500–$2,000 plumbing repair. Have a professional assess the shutoff valves and supply lines before attempting any faucet work in a home older than 30 years.

4 Gutter Cleaning and Fascia Repair

Falls from ladders are one of the leading causes of serious home injury in the US — and gutter work requires leaning a ladder against a roofline and moving it repeatedly. Beyond the safety issue, Austin's pecan and live oak trees are prolific. Gutters clog fast, and water backup under fascia boards is a primary cause of soffit rot and attic moisture damage. A professional cleaning + inspection costs $75–$125 and takes about 45 minutes. The peace of mind — and the check that fascia isn't already showing damage — is worth every dollar.

5 Anything Involving Your Electrical Panel

In Texas, work on wired electrical systems requires a licensed electrician for unpermitted work in most jurisdictions. But even setting aside the legal issue: amateurs working inside electrical panels are responsible for a significant percentage of residential house fires in the US. If you are replacing a switch, that is a straightforward DIY task. If you are replacing a breaker, adding a circuit, or addressing an overloaded panel — call a licensed professional. The risk-reward ratio does not favor doing it yourself.

Smart DIY vs. Smart Hire: A Quick Guide

  • Fine to DIY: Caulking, minor patching, replacing light fixtures, installing ceiling fans (if existing wiring is in good shape), hanging pictures and shelves, changing out cabinet hardware
  • Usually hire out: Drywall texture matching, faucet replacement in older homes, GFCI outlet installation, smoke detector wiring, gutter cleaning and inspection, door frame repair
  • Always hire out: Electrical panel work, load-bearing wall modifications, anything involving gas lines, major plumbing in older homes
